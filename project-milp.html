A Microgrid Approach to Energy Cost and Emission Reduction for Sustainable Port Operations

Institution: Newcastle University & Singapore Institute of Technology
In collaboration with: Power Automation Pte Ltd & NUS (National University of Singapore)
Supervisor: Dr. Tan Kuan Tak
Tools & Techniques: Python, MILP, Hierarchical Optimization, ESS, Solar PV, HOMER

This project developed a Python-based energy management system for Jurong Port to reduce operational costs and carbon emissions through a microgrid architecture. A hierarchical MILP algorithm managed grid interaction, energy storage, and load shedding, integrating real-world solar PV data.

Key Contributions

  • Developed and validated a multi-layer MILP optimization algorithm
  • Achieved up to 60% grid independence via solar integration
  • Realized cost savings exceeding $15,000 using ESS
  • Benchmarked results against HOMER software and peer models
  • Exposed scheduling-performance gaps between offline and online MPC control

Methodology

The MILP formulation minimized total cost over a scheduling horizon. The core objective function is:

Minimize: ∑t ( Cgrid(t) * Pgrid(t) + Ccharge * Pch(t) + Cdischarge * Pdis(t) )

Subject to constraints:

  • Energy balance: Pload(t) = Pgrid(t) + Pdis(t) - Pch(t) - PPV(t)
  • Battery state of charge (SOC) dynamics
  • Power limits and PV generation availability

A hierarchical strategy separated long-term scheduling (macro level) from short-term adjustments (micro level), improving computational efficiency.

Test Validation

Simulation data was analyzed to verify the algorithm's robustness.

PV, Load, and Price Inputs

1. Generation Analysis

When PV generation increased from 300 kWh to 500 kWh, cost savings increased significantly due to reduced grid dependence. The algorithm successfully captured the economic benefits of higher renewable penetration.

2. Charging/Discharging Cost Optimization

Charging and discharging actions were aligned with the dynamic tariff structure, demonstrating that cost functions were correctly applied and reflected in optimized dispatch.

3. Energy Storage System (ESS) Contribution

With ESS installed, the system optimized energy arbitrage across low and high tariff periods, generating additional revenue. The operation also helped minimize load curtailment.
