Self-Guided Inductive Vehicle with Embedded Control Systems

Institution: Singapore Institute of Technology
Supervisor: Dr. Tan Kuan Tak
Tools & Techniques: Python, Mixed-Integer Linear Programming (MILP), Hierarchical Optimization, Energy Storage Systems (ESS), Solar PV, HOMER Software

This project developed a Python-based energy management system for Jurong Port to reduce operational costs and carbon emissions through a microgrid architecture. Using a hierarchical MILP algorithm, the system coordinated energy storage, grid interaction, and load management while incorporating real-world solar PV generation data.

Key Contributions

  • Developed and validated a multi-layer MILP optimization algorithm for microgrid control
  • Integrated solar PV to achieve up to 60% grid independence
  • Demonstrated operational cost savings of over $15,000 with ESS
  • Validated results against HOMER software and external benchmark datasets
  • Highlighted gaps between offline scheduling and MPC-based online operation
